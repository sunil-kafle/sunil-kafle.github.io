Airport Delay Performance Analytics

An analytical project focused on evaluating flight delay performance across U.S. airports and airlines, enabling high-level benchmarking and operational performance assessment using delay metrics over time.

Overview

Airport Delay Performance Analytics was developed to address the challenge of understanding and comparing flight delay behavior across U.S. airports and airlines using operational data. Flight delays impact passengers, airlines, and airport operations, yet delay patterns are often buried in fragmented reports and averages that mask variability. The goal of this project is to provide a clear, consolidated view of delay performance that supports benchmarking, monitoring, and high-level assessment of operational reliability across airports and carriers.

Approach

The project uses structured flight delay data to analyze performance across airports and airlines over time. Key delay metrics were aggregated and segmented by delay type to enable comparison and trend analysis. Interactive visualizations were built in Tableau using calculated fields, time-series views, and benchmarking logic, allowing users to explore delay behavior, compare performance across entities, and monitor changes through dynamic filters.

Results

The analysis revealed meaningful variation in delay performance across airports and airlines, with clear differences in both magnitude and consistency over time. Time-series views highlighted periods of elevated delay and variability, while benchmarking enabled identification of underperforming and above-average performers. Breaking delays down by type provided additional context on whether issues were driven by operational factors, weather, or downstream aircraft availability, supporting more informed performance assessment.

Tools & Technologies

  • Tableau
  • Calculated Fields and Table Calculations
  • Time-Series Analysis
  • Performance Benchmarking
  • Interactive Filters and Parameters
